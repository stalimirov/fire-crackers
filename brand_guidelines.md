# Fire Crackers — Brand Guidelines

---

## 1. Brand Strategy

### Mission
To make the boldest, most unapologetic snack in the room — crafted from real Bulgarian ingredients, built around characters worth following, and never, ever bland.

### Brand Values
- **Bold** — No half measures. Every product, every piece of content, every design decision commits.
- **Craft** — Few ingredients, locally sourced, made with intent. The opposite of anonymous factory food.
- **Irreverent** — Doesn't take itself seriously. Winks at the audience. Earns the 18+ mark with attitude, not just heat.
- **Authentic** — Bulgarian roots, global aesthetic. Never pretends to be something it isn't.
- **Character** — The brand has a cast, a world, a story. Not just a product.

### Positioning Statement
Fire Crackers is the first craft hot snack brand built for adults who are tired of beige. Made in Bulgaria from locally sourced ingredients, sold in bars, worn on t-shirts, and followed online. The snack is real. The universe is the point.

### Brand Personality
If Fire Crackers were a person: confident without being a try-hard, funny without needing to explain the joke, slightly unhinged in the best way, deeply loyal to quality, and the kind of person who turns up to a party with something nobody expected.

## 2. Voice & Tone

### Language
**English-first, everywhere.** Social media, packaging copy, merch, content, character names — all English.

**Bulgarian as a deliberate wink.** Occasional Bulgarian words, phrases, or Cyrillic used as a cultural signal — not as a fallback, but as a confident nod to origin. Used sparingly and intentionally, like a signature. Legal/allergen labels are bilingual as required by EU regulation.

### Voice Principles

**Short and punchy.** The brand doesn't ramble. One good line beats three okay ones.

**Irreverent but not reckless.** Cheeky, adult, self-aware. Never mean. Never dangerous-for-the-sake-of-dangerous. "Snack Responsibly" is a wink, not a warning label.

**Character-led.** Copy can be written from a character's voice when appropriate. Blaze would say something different from Shipka. Use that.

**Never corporate.** No synergy. No journey. No passion for excellence. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.

### Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Social content | Chaotic, funny, fast |
| Packaging | Bold, confident, a little smug |
| Merch | Iconic, minimal, let the art speak |
| PR / media | Still irreverent, slightly more coherent |
| Legal / labels | Clear, compliant, neutral |

### Writing Rules
- Use contractions. "We're" not "we are."
- Active voice always.
- Sentences can be fragments. On purpose.
- Rhetorical questions are fine. Answering them immediately is better.
- Avoid: "premium," "artisan," "passionate," "journey," "crafted with love." Show it, don't say it.
- The 18+ and "Snack Responsibly" marks are brand statements, not disclaimers — treat them that way in copy.

## 3. Visual Identity

### Art Style
The visual foundation is built on **Mid-Century Print Mascot** aesthetics rather than classic rubber-hose animation.

- Thick and consistent black outlines define the form of every character to ensure they remain iconic at any scale.
- Visible halftone and stipple dots are the primary method for creating depth and shadows within the illustrations.
- Design focuses on holistic Brand Lockups where bold typography and characters are tightly integrated into a single unit.
- A distressed Risograph or rubber-stamp texture is applied to the final art to create an authentic vintage print feel.
- Character expressions favor a smug or unbothered cool over manic or hyper-active animation energy.
- Solid background colors in either high-vibration primaries or earthy vintage tones are used to anchor the compositions.
- Anatomy remains simplified with white gloves and chunky footwear used as a functional nod to classic mascot heritage.
- Final assets must look like they were pulled directly from a 1960s matchbook or a weathered bar coaster.

This art style is the brand's most valuable visual asset. All illustration, packaging art, and merch design must be executed within this language. No flat modern illustration. No 3D rendering. No clean digital finishes.

### Tattoo Culture Alignment

The brand's visual language is not coincidentally close to American Traditional and Neo-Traditional tattoo aesthetics — it shares the same foundations: bold black outlines, flat color fills, limited palette, iconic imagery that reads at small scale, timeless design that doesn't date. This is structural, not decorative.

**Why this matters:**

The primary bar audience — craft beer drinkers, metal fans, urban nightlife regulars — has significant overlap with tattoo culture. These are not separate tribes with separate aesthetics. The same person who has a Sailor Jerry flash piece probably also collects vintage matchbook art. Fire Crackers sits naturally at that intersection.

**Design implication:** Every character must be designed to work as tattoo flash. This means:
- Clean silhouette readable at 5cm
- No detail too fine to survive reduction to skin scale
- Color palette matches what works in traditional tattooing (reds, blacks, creams, golds)
- Composition works as a standalone piece without brand context

**Brand implication:** Tattoo culture is a cultural reference point, not a marketing tactic. The brand doesn't chase tattoo culture — it belongs to the same tradition. That's a different, more credible position.

**Tattoo flash sheet:** A dedicated asset — all 8 characters laid out as a traditional flash sheet (single A2 or A3 page, characters arranged as they would be on a tattoo studio wall) — is both a merch item (sold as a print) and the single best demonstration that the character designs are genuinely tattoo-worthy, not just tattoo-adjacent.

**Collaborations:** Tattoo artists who work in the American Traditional or Neo-Traditional style are natural collaborators — for limited edition packaging variants, for content, and as organic brand ambassadors within a community that does not respond to paid promotion the way other audiences do.

### Typography

**Display Hierarchy**
Custom hand-lettered or heavily distorted display type is the primary brand identifier. The Fire Crackers wordmark is drawn, not typed — heavy weight, slightly uneven, feels like it was stamped or branded into the surface.

Headlines must be ultra-bold with rounded terminals to match the thick character outlines. Final font TBD with designer — candidates include Grandstander Bold, Baloo 2 ExtraBold, or similar Google Fonts with rounded, heavy weights. Used for product names, flavor names, callouts, and packaging hierarchy.

Typography is treated as a physical object within a Brand Lockup — often curving, arching, or wrapping around the character. High-contrast color pairings from the brand palette (e.g. Aged Cream text on Fire Red or Ember Orange backgrounds) are required for readability in lockup contexts.

**Supporting Styles**
`Barlow Condensed Bold` — Google Fonts, free. Clean, heavy sans-serif used as the sub-brand font for descriptors within lockups (e.g. flavor descriptors, taglines, secondary copy). Tight, strong, never competes with the display type.

**Body / Label Copy**
`DM Sans Regular / Medium` — Google Fonts, free. Clean, readable at small sizes for ingredients, allergen info, and legal text.

**Type Rules:**
- All-caps is encouraged for display use — but not mandatory
- Tracking in lockup contexts: tight (-25 to -50) so the text block functions as a single graphic element
- Tracking for standalone display text (stamps, letterpress effects): loose (+50 to +100) for a pressed/branded feel
- Normal tracking on body copy
- Distressed textures — ink traps, rounded-off corners, slight erosion — are applied to all display type. No clean digital edges.
- Bulgarian Cyrillic display type must follow the same ultra-bold, rounded weights as the English display type. Body/legal Cyrillic: use `DM Sans` or `Noto Sans` Cyrillic variants.
- Never use fonts outside this system without explicit sign-off

### Logo System

Four versions for different applications:

1. **Primary Wordmark** — "Fire Crackers" hand-lettered with Blaze icon integrated or adjacent
2. **Stacked Lockup** — Wordmark above tagline "Snack Responsibly" in Barlow Condensed
3. **Seal / Stamp** — Circular logo for wax seal, embossing, bottle caps, badges. Contains abbreviated mark and tagline around the edge.
4. **Icon Only** — Blaze head only, simplified for favicon, pin, small applications

Minimum size: 24px / 8mm for icon. Never display below this threshold.

Clear space: minimum of the cap-height of the wordmark on all sides.

---

### Texture & Finish

Every piece of brand output — digital or print — carries texture. Everything should look like it came off a vintage print press, not a screen.

- **Halftone and stipple dot overlay** at 15–30% opacity on all illustrations — the primary tool for depth and shadow
- **Risograph registration offset** — slight color misalignment between layers to simulate multi-pass printing
- **Ink bleed** at edges of filled areas — nothing has a perfectly clean boundary
- **Burn/scorch marks** as decorative graphic elements — edges of packaging, section dividers in content
- **Rough edges** on any bordered elements — never perfect geometric shapes
- **Paper texture** on packaging — the kraft stock is part of the visual identity, not just a material choice

In digital contexts (social, web), these textures are baked into the assets. Nothing looks screen-fresh.

---

### Photography & Mixed Media

When real-world photography is used (brand content, bar placement, events):

- **Illustrated overlay on live action** is the primary approach: static character art composited over real footage, with subtle Live2D motion (breathing, blinks) where budget allows. Protects identity of people featured. Makes all content immediately identifiable as Fire Crackers.
- Illustrated sweat, fire, explosion effects added in post over real reactions
- Blaze or other characters can appear as illustrated overlays in real environments
- Live action footage: warm, slightly desaturated, high contrast. Shot on real cameras not phone vertical where possible. Print-style texture added in post.
- No stock photography. Ever.

## 4. Character Guidelines

### General Principles

Characters are designed with a posing-first mentality — they should look like static mid-century mascots, not captured frames of animation.

- Facial expressions default to smug smirks, half-lidded eyes, or deadpan side-eye rather than hyper-active grins.
- Heavy use of sunglasses is encouraged to enhance the unbothered persona of the flagship characters.
- Poses utilize the cool lean or the relaxed hand-on-hip to convey confidence and adult-oriented irreverence.
- Every character features a thick and consistent outline that ensures a strong silhouette even with minimalist detailing.
- Visible stipple and halftone shading are the primary tools for adding depth to character bodies.

### Character Hierarchy

Blaze is the face. He appears on the primary SKU, the core brand identity, and leads all top-level marketing. The rest of the cast are full characters — not mascots for single products. They exist in the brand universe independently of which SKU they anchor.

### Character Roster & Visual Specs

---

**BLAZE**
*Chili pepper — Main character*

Visual: Classic red chili pepper body, burning wick on top (the fuse), thick black outlines, white four-fingered gloved hands, smug smirk that suggests he knows a joke you don't. Eyes are large and round — expressive even in static poses. The wick flame varies with context.

Color family: Fire Red `#CC2200`, Char Black `#1C1410`, Aged Cream `#F2E4C4` for gloves and eyes.

Personality in visuals: The peak of smug confidence. Always in a cool lean, always slightly too confident for the situation. Default pose: one hand on hip, wick blazing, half-lidded eyes.

SKU anchor: Core wheat cracker, flagship product.

---

**MATCHSTICK**
*Soleti breadstick — The rookie*

Visual: Long, thin silhouette. Head is the matchstick tip — pale yellow-white at rest, ignites when excited or startled. Spindly limbs, smaller than everyone else. Always positioned slightly behind Blaze.

Color family: Pale Sulphur `#E8E0A0`, Ember Orange `#E8621A` (when lit), Char Black.

Personality in visuals: The judgmental rookie — dry-humored contrast to the group. Default expression: bored eyes, unimpressed side-eye. His thin silhouette provides visual contrast against the heavier characters.

SKU anchor: Soleti line.

---

**HA-HANK**
*Habanero — The muscle*

Visual: Big, rounded habanero body — wider than tall, like he's been fed well. Thick limbs, wide stance, heavy-lidded knowing smile. Hat optional but plausible. Built for power — always looks relaxed until the moment he isn't.

Color family: Deep Orange-Red `#D45000`, Ember Orange `#E8621A`, Char Black.

Personality in visuals: The relaxed giant. Default pose: arms wide open, completely at ease, knowing smile. At rest = easygoing. In action = unstoppable.

SKU anchor: Habanero SKU.

---

**CAROLINA**
*Carolina Reaper pepper — The queen*

Visual: Tall, elegant, the scorpion tail of the Reaper pepper stylized into a dramatic curved element — worn like a crown or a train. Sharp, deliberate lines compared to the rounder characters — a pointy silhouette that communicates danger through elegant amusement. Posed with intention. Expression: perpetually and mildly amused. Knows exactly what she's capable of. Never needs to prove it.

Color family: Crimson `#991100`, Deep Crimson into near-black at the tail, Aged Cream accents.

Personality in visuals: Upright posture, composed. The only character who never looks caught off guard. Default pose: standing still, looking at something slightly off-frame as if she already knew you were coming.

SKU anchor: Carolina Reaper / extreme heat SKU.

---

**GHOST**
*Ghost pepper — The mystery*

Visual: Semi-transparent — the only character rendered with visible transparency layers. Pale, slightly luminous. No fixed lower body — trails off into vapor. Eyes are present but hollow, a deadpan unimpressed stare seen through fog. Appears in the background of compositions before anyone notices.

Color family: Ghost White `#E8EBE4`, Pale Blue-Grey `#C5CDD0`, Char Black outlines but lighter weight than other characters.

Personality in visuals: The deadpan mystery. Barely there. Default pose: standing just behind someone who doesn't know he's there yet, hollow stare directed at the viewer.

SKU anchor: Ghost pepper SKU, extreme heat line.

---

**ABI**
*Wasabi peas — The strategist, female*

Visual: Small, round pea body with a sharp, precise aesthetic defined by perfectly symmetrical lines. Deep green tones. Expression default: stoic gaze, focused, evaluating. Unlike the pepper characters, her design leans slightly more geometric — the retro style adapted to a crisper visual language that suits her personality. Wears a simple band or wrap that reads as functional rather than decorative.

Color family: Deep Green `#2D5016`, Wasabi Green `#7DB050`, Char Black.

Personality in visuals: The geometric strategist. Default pose: arms folded, slightly turned, thinking. Every gesture is deliberate and economical — the most still character in any scene.

SKU anchor: Wasabi peas SKU.

---

**SHIPKA**
*Bulgarian Shipka pepper — The elder*

Visual: Stocky, weathered, broad-shouldered. Scars on his surface that he's never explained and nobody asks about. Older proportions — heavier lines, more gravitas in the face. A kind but stoic expression by default — eyes that have seen things, with a weathered and unmoving gravity. Occasionally has a hand on someone else's shoulder — the mentor gesture.

Color family: Wax Gold `#C9933A`, Warm Red-Brown `#8B3A2A`, Char Black with heavier outline weight than younger characters.

Personality in visuals: The stoic cultural anchor. Settled. Never surprised. Default pose: standing straight, arms at sides, holding direct eye contact with the viewer. The only character who addresses the audience.

SKU anchor: Bulgarian Shipka pepper SKU. The local pride edition.

Special note: Shipka is the brand's cultural anchor. His design should carry visible connection to Bulgarian visual heritage — subtle references in pattern or detail are appropriate and encouraged.

---

**SMOKY**
*Smoked paprika — The specialist*

Visual: Always partially obscured by his own smoke — the smoke is not a special effect, it is part of his design. Deep burgundy-brown tones. Leans against things. The smoke drifts in every composition. Expression: half-lidded eyes, slight smile, permanently and definitively unbothered.

Color family: Smoke `#7A6E5F`, Deep Burgundy `#5C1F1F`, Kraft `#C4956A`.

Personality in visuals: The most visually still character. While others are posed mid-attitude, Smoky is posed mid-thought. Default pose: leaning against a wall or surface, smoke drifting, not looking at whatever everyone else is looking at. A total lack of rush.

SKU anchor: Smoked paprika SKU. No rush on this one — Smoky is brand equity first, product second.

---

### Character Usage Rules

- Characters are always drawn in the established retro illustration style. No exceptions for "quick" social content — a simplified but on-style version is preferred over an off-style detailed one.
- Characters do not endorse real-world products or brands outside Fire Crackers.
- Characters can appear together. When they do, Blaze leads unless the scene specifically calls for another character.
- Ghost should appear unexpectedly in group scenes — in the background, partially behind something, before he's formally introduced to the viewer.
- Carolina and Blaze's dynamic (his oblivious rivalry, her calm disregard) is a recurring story engine. Lean into it.
- Shipka does not participate in chaos. He observes it, sometimes intervenes, never causes it.

## 5. Packaging Guidelines

### Format
Small to medium bags — bar-friendly sizes. Primary format: kraft paper stand-up pouch with heat-sealed top and wax-stamped closure.

### Mandatory Packaging Elements
Every pack must include, without exception:
- Fire Crackers wordmark
- Character name and illustration (one character per SKU)
- Flavor name in headline display font
- Heat tier name + flame count (stamped style)
- Ingredient list (Bulgarian + English)
- Allergen declaration (14 EU allergens, highlighted per regulation)
- Net weight
- Producer information + BFSA registration
- Best before date location indicated
- "Snack Responsibly" — front or back, visible
- 18+ mark — front, visible
- "Made in Bulgaria" with origin indicator

### Packaging Layout Principle
Front face: Character illustration (dominant) + flavor name + heat tier. Bold, little text, let the illustration carry it.
Back face: Ingredients, allergens, legal, producer info, a short line of copy in brand voice.

### Wax Seal
Red wax seal on the sealed top edge of the bag. Embossed with the circular seal logo. Hand-applied at launch scale. This is the maker's mark — the craft signal. It should look like it was done by a person, not a machine.

### SKU Color Coding
Each SKU carries a secondary accent color tied to its character. The kraft base stays consistent across all SKUs — the character and the accent color differentiate them at shelf.

## 6. Content & Motion Style

### Static Illustration — Primary Output
All brand content defaults to print-quality static illustration. Every social post, packaging asset, and merch design is built as a finished print piece first. If it doesn't work as a still image, it doesn't ship.

### Live2D Motion Layer
Subtle motion applied to static art for social and digital contexts — not traditional frame-by-frame animation.

- **Approach:** Breathing, blinks, parallax, gentle sway — the character feels alive without being redrawn per frame.
- **Frame rate:** 12fps for any motion (maintains the hand-drawn, "on twos" feel).
- **Backgrounds:** Static or slow parallax pan, textured, worn — never smooth digital scrolls.
- **Constraint:** Live2D motion enhances the static art. If the motion would require redrawing the character, it's out of scope for current production.

### Traditional Animation — Future Aspiration
Full frame-by-frame animation (mini-series, long-form shorts) is a Stage 2+ goal. When it happens, it will be executed within the mid-century print mascot style — not rubber-hose or Fleischer revival. Until then, static illustration and Live2D carry the brand.

### Sound Design
When video content is produced:
- Vintage cartoon sound effects — spring boing, old-school explosion, tinny brass hits
- Music: original jazzy/ragtime-influenced themes per character where applicable
- Duration: Social shorts 5–30 seconds. Longer-form content 2–5 minutes.

### Mixed Media Rules
When combining real footage with illustration:
- Illustrated character faces replace real faces (not just overlaid on top — they replace)
- Illustrated effects (fire, sweat, explosions, stars) react to real events in the footage, with optional Live2D motion
- Colour grade the live footage to sit in the same warm, high-contrast, print-textured world as the illustrations
- The seam between real and illustrated should be intentional and visible — the technique is part of the aesthetic, not a flaw to hide

### Content Tone
Wild. Absurdist. Fast. Serialized where possible. Short-form social content and any future long-form series share the same universe — events in one can reference events in the other. The audience who follows both gets rewarded.

## 7. Merch Guidelines

### Design Principle
Merch carries characters and brand marks — not product messaging. A Fire Crackers t-shirt is something someone wears because the art is good, not because they want to advertise crackers.

### Approved Merch Applications
- T-shirts: character illustration or wordmark. Black, off-white, or kraft-tone base only.
- Beanies: embroidered patch of seal logo or simplified character head
- Tote bags: large format single-character illustration, minimal text
- Enamel pins: simplified character icon at 25–40mm
- Beer mugs: character flat design, stamped-style wordmark
- Coasters: single character per coaster, set of 4–8 covers the core cast
- Stickers: character expressions, logo, flame icons — designed for sticker-sheet format

### Merch Rules
- No photorealistic rendering on merch — retro illustration only
- Wordmark on merch is always the custom hand-lettered version, never typeset
- Characters on merch should be recognizable at small scale — test at thumbnail size
- Limited edition merch drops tied to character or seasonal flavor launches
- Merch should be desirable as standalone objects — if it wouldn't sell without the Fire Crackers name, the design isn't strong enough

---

## 8. What Fire Crackers Is Not

To protect the brand as it grows:

- Not a health food brand. Do not add wellness claims that compromise the identity.
- Not a children's brand. 18+ mark is a statement of positioning, not just a suggestion.
- Not a discount brand. Do not participate in heavy price promotions that undermine craft positioning.
- Not a generic hot sauce brand. The product category is crackers/snacks. Adjacent hot sauce territory is separate and requires separate brand thinking.
- Not a corporate brand. As Fire Crackers grows, the voice, the irreverence, and the character-led identity must survive. The moment it sounds like a press release, something has gone wrong.
