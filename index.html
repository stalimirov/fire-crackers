05 — Art Style & Visual DNA

Mid-Century Print Mascot
meets Sailor Jerry
meets tattoo flash.

Three reference points. One coherent visual language. All three share the same structural DNA: thick black outlines, flat color fills, limited palette, bold iconography that reads at any scale.

01
Posing-first mascots. Thick, consistent black outlines. Halftone and stipple dots for depth. Smug smirks, half-lidded eyes, unbothered cool — never manic or hyper-active. Static mid-century print feel.
02
Everything looks slightly aged. Film grain overlays. Halftone dot patterns in shadow zones. Scorch marks as graphic elements. Nothing looks screen-fresh. The brand has history before it launched.
03
Tattoo-flash ready. Every character must read cleanly at 5cm. Bold silhouette, limited palette, no detail too fine for ink. This is structural — not decorative. The bar audience and the tattoo audience are the same person.
Primary references
  • 🏷️ 1950s–60s matchbook & bar coaster mascots
  • 💉 Sailor Jerry — American Traditional flash sheets
  • 🖨️ Risograph & rubber-stamp print texture
  • 🎨 Mid-century advertising illustration — bold, iconic, print-first
Non-negotiable

No flat modern illustration. No 3D rendering. No clean digital finishes. Final assets must look like they were pulled from a 1960s matchbook or a weathered bar coaster. This is the brand's most valuable visual asset.

Venue tiers — phased rollout by type

Tier 1 — Core
Stage 1, Sofia: Craft beer bars (40–50), board game cafes (10–15), cocktail bars (25–35), specialty food shops (15–25). Board game cafes have highest dwell time and near-perfect brand fit (character-driven, visual-culture audience).
Tier 2 — Expand
Stages 1–2: Wine bars (45–65 national), co-working spaces (40–55), hookah bars mid-premium (60–85). Bulgarian meze culture means snack + drink pairing is already native — no market education needed.
Tier 3 — Growth
Stage 2+: Gastropubs, escape rooms, boutique hotels (€4–5 minibar markup), festivals & events (200–500 packs/day), alt. cinemas. Total addressable: 700–900 venues nationally, 350–500 in Sofia.

Sale-or-Return Terms

Terms

30-day return window — after 30 days, venue owns the stock.
Payment on reorder — first delivery is float; paid when they order again.
Minimum reorder: 10 packs — below that, venue isn't worth servicing.
Cut at 20 packs/mo — if a venue can't move 20/mo after 6 weeks, it's the wrong fit.

Phase out

After 3 successful reorders → move to standard wholesale (payment on delivery). The relationship is proven — no safety net needed.

Expected return rate: 5–10% at Tier 1 venues. Impact: effective COGS rises ~€0.08/pack. Margin drops from 52% to ~46%. Manageable.

Bootstrappable — by design

No external investor needed for Stage 1. All founders work for equity — zero salary overhead. No HACCP consultant needed (kitchen is already certified). No co-packer until month 7–10 when revenue justifies it. Ongoing overhead: accountant (~€50/mo), delivery runs (~€30/mo), liability insurance (~€25/mo).

Legally required even as distributor: batch traceability log (1-page spreadsheet per batch — supplier, date, quantity, who received it) and a recall procedure document. Both are lightweight but non-negotiable under EU Reg 178/2002. Shared kitchen also requires an allergen cross-contamination note on the label if the facility handles common allergens.

Contract production model

We never own production risk

Fire Crackers registers as a brand owner and food distributor, not a food producer. Label reads: "Produced by [Kitchen/Co-packer] for Fire Crackers OOD."

This means: no HACCP plan creation, no facility inspections, no temperature monitoring logs, no supplier audit records. The certified kitchen (Stage 1) and co-packer (Stage 2) carry all production compliance.

Risk Register

Eight real risks.
Specific responses.

COGS Higher Than Modeled

No co-packer quotes yet. €0.55 is an industry estimate. Pepper prices fluctuate seasonally.

Response: Start at ~€0.80 handmade — model already works. Get 3 co-packer quotes before Stage 2. Lock suppliers early.

Trigger: best quote above €0.90 → raise wholesale or reduce pack.

Venues Don't Reorder

Sale-or-return = zero commitment. Product can sit untouched and get quietly returned.

Response: Weekly venue visits in months 1–2. Track per-venue sell-through from day one. Cut venues under 20 packs/mo after 6 weeks.

Trigger: fewer than 2 of first 5 venues reorder within 6 weeks → reformulate before scaling.

Chilli Hills Launches Crackers

They have bar relationships, supply chain, brand, and proven demand (chips sold out). Cracker SKU is a natural extension.

Response: Move fast — first-mover window is 6–12 months. Target venues they're not in. Brand IP is the long-term moat.

Trigger: cracker announcement → accelerate co-packer, shift marketing to brand differentiation.

Content Quality Too Low at ~€10/mo

Brand promise is mid-century print mascot visuals — bold, iconic, print-quality. Gap between pitch art direction and €10/mo tools is real.

Response: Designer is a co-founder (equity). Static illustration + Live2D — puppet-style suits mid-century print aesthetic. Mini-series deferred to Stage 2.

Trigger: first 20 posts underperform comparable BG food brand accounts → rethink visual approach.

Team Burns Out (3 Unpaid People)

All equity, no salary. Everyone has other obligations. Equity motivation fades when work is unglamorous.

Response: Hard deadline — if not €500+/mo revenue by month 8, each person reassesses. Distribution hire (month 6–7) offloads delivery logistics.

Trigger: any member pulls back → redistribute immediately. One dropout without a plan kills everything.

Seasonal Demand Drops

Bulgarian bar traffic drops Nov–Feb except indoor venues. Summer terraces are weather-dependent.

Response: Venue mix — board game cafes, co-working, specialty shops are counter-seasonal. D2C bundles fill winter gaps. Don't batch-produce in November.

Trigger: sell-through below 50% of peak for 2+ months → too concentrated in seasonal venues.

Kitchen Access Falls Through

Friend's certified kitchen is the cheapest production path, but availability is unconfirmed. Our production blocks their use of the kitchen — not a long-term arrangement.

Response: Formalize arrangement in writing before launch. Identify 1 backup commercial kitchen with pricing. A fallback at €200–400/day adds €800–1,600 to Stage 1 budget.

Trigger: friend can't commit to 5+ days → find alternative before first batch. Budget worst case.

€1.45 Wholesale Rejected by Venues

Venues currently pay €0.25–0.65 for mass snacks. €1.45 is Chilli Hills territory — but they're an established brand. We're unknown.

Response: Test price in first 3 venue conversations. If pushback, consider €1.20–1.30 introductory wholesale for first 5 venues (lower margin, faster adoption). Raise after proven sell-through.

Trigger: 2 of 3 venue owners hesitate on price → adjust before committing to 1,000-pack batch.
