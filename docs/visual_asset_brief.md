## Style Reference Guide

### Primary references (study these closely):
- **Cuphead** (MDHR Studio, 2017) — the single closest reference for execution quality, outline weight, color approach, and character construction. Every character in Cuphead is a useful study.
- **Fleischer Studios, 1930–1941** — Betty Boop, Popeye, Out of the Inkwell series. Source material for rubber-hose construction and the slightly unsettling expressiveness.
- **Disney Silly Symphonies, 1929–1939** — background texture, color palette approach, the warmth underneath the chaos.
- **American Traditional tattoo flash** — Sailor Jerry Collins, Norman Collins, Owen Jensen. Study the line weight consistency, the color discipline, the way each design reads as a complete composition at small scale. The brand characters must hold up to this standard. If a character design wouldn't work as a flash piece on a tattoo studio wall, it isn't finished yet.

### What makes this style:
- **Outlines:** Thick, consistent-weight black outlines (3–5px at standard canvas size). Outlines are slightly imperfect — they are hand-drawn, not auto-stroked.
- **Color fills:** Flat color within outlines. No gradients except where a gradient is textured with grain (rendering a shadow or a glow). No smooth digital gradients.
- **Whites:** The whites of eyes, gloves, and teeth are never pure #FFFFFF. Use Aged Cream `#F2E4C4` or a slightly dirty off-white that feels period-appropriate.
- **Shadows:** Cel-shaded, hard-edge, one shadow layer. Color-matched to the fill color, darkened and shifted slightly warm.
- **Film grain:** Every finished asset has a grain overlay at 15–25% opacity. This is non-negotiable. Assets without grain look wrong.
- **Halftone:** Background areas and large shadow zones use halftone dot patterns, not solid fills.
- **Line quality:** The line has life — it thickens slightly at corners and tapers at tips. It should feel like it was drawn with a brush, not plotted by a computer.

### What to avoid:
- Modern flat design aesthetics (no thin lines, no minimal geometry)
- Any form of smooth gradient without grain
- 3D rendering or pseudo-3D shading
- Clean, sharp vector auto-strokes
- Overly cute or childlike proportions — this style is retro but the characters are adults
- Pure black (`#000000`) or pure white (`#FFFFFF`) anywhere in the final art

## Tier 1 Assets

### 1.1 — Brand Wordmark

**What it is:** The hand-lettered "Fire Crackers" wordmark. This is the single most important asset in the project. Everything else is built around it.

**Style direction:**
The lettering should feel like it was burned or branded into a surface — not written on it. Heavy strokes, slightly uneven, organic. Think a cross between:
- Old American circus poster lettering (bold, slightly condensed, showman energy)
- A woodblock print stamp (slightly imperfect impression, ink variation)
- A cattle brand (burned, permanent, no decorative excess)

The letterforms are slab serif in character but drawn, not typed. No existing font should be detectable underneath. This is custom lettering.

**Feeling:** Authority. Permanence. Slightly dangerous. Like something you'd find stamped on a crate that shouldn't be opened.

**Characteristics:**
- Weight: heavy. The strokes are thick and confident.
- Width: slightly condensed — occupies horizontal space efficiently
- Caps or mixed case: mixed case preferred, with a large F and large C, remaining letters sized dynamically
- No swashes, no excessive flourishes — clean and mean
- Fire or heat integration: subtle. A single flame emerging from a letterform is acceptable. The lettering itself should feel hot without requiring a fire emoji drawn onto it.
- The two words can sit on one line or stack — both versions needed

**Deliverables:**
- Wordmark, one line, horizontal layout
- Wordmark, stacked (Fire above Crackers)
- Wordmark on dark background (Char Black base, Aged Cream lettering)
- Wordmark on light background (Aged Cream / Kraft base, Char Black lettering)
- Wordmark on transparent background (PNG and vector)
- All at minimum 2000px wide at 300dpi

**File formats:** AI (primary), SVG, PNG (transparent background, dark version, light version)

### 1.2 — Seal / Stamp Design

**What it is:** A circular brand stamp used for the wax seal, embossing, small applications, and as a standalone icon. The "maker's mark."

**Structure:**
- Outer ring: circular text reading "FIRE CRACKERS" at top arc, "SNACK RESPONSIBLY" at bottom arc, small flame separators at 3 and 9 o'clock positions
- Inner circle: simplified Blaze head icon (front-facing, grinning, wick burning) — or the wordmark mark condensed
- Border: double-rule circle, slightly imperfect, stamp-pressed feel
- Overall diameter at standard use: can be reproduced at 20mm (for wax seal) without losing legibility

**Feeling:** A whiskey distillery seal or a Victorian apothecary stamp. Serious but slightly ominous.

**Deliverables:**
- Full seal, black on transparent
- Full seal, Aged Cream on Char Black (for dark embossing)
- Full seal, Fire Red (for wax seal reference)
- Physical die reference: confirm the design works when embossed — no detail too fine to survive physical pressing at 20mm

**File formats:** AI, SVG, PNG (all color variants)

### 1.3 — Blaze Character Sheet

**What he is:** Red chili pepper, main character, burning wick, white four-fingered gloves, wide permanent grin.

**Body construction:**
- Head: large, round at top, tapers to the classic chili point at bottom. The point is the chin. Eyes sit in the upper half.
- Eyes: large, round, white sclera (Aged Cream), dark iris, high-shine spot highlight. The eyes carry all his emotional range — they grow, shrink, tilt, go wide. They are the most expressive part of him.
- Mouth: wide, open grin showing teeth. The grin rarely changes shape — it is his resting expression. What changes is the eyes around it.
- Wick: emerges from the top of his head like a natural extension of the pepper stem. Slightly curved. The flame at the tip is his emotional barometer — small and steady when calm, large and erratic when excited or angry.
- Arms: rubber-hose construction. No sharp elbows. They curve and bend naturally with the movement without anatomical constraints.
- Hands: four-fingered, white gloves (Cuphead standard). Slightly oversized relative to his arms.
- Legs: short, sturdy, rubber-hose. He stands in a wide, confident stance by default.
- Body: the pepper body is the torso. He does not wear clothing. The pepper skin IS his body.

**Color:**
- Body: Fire Red `#CC2200`
- Shadow on body: Deep Crimson `#991100`, hard cel-shaded edge
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410`
- Wick: warm brown `#5C3A1A`
- Flame: Ember Orange `#E8621A` core, Fire Red `#CC2200` outer, Aged Cream `#F2E4C4` hottest center
- Gloves: Aged Cream `#F2E4C4`
- Eyes (sclera): Aged Cream `#F2E4C4`
- Eyes (iris): Char Black `#1C1410`

**Required poses:**
1. **Hero pose** — confident lean, one hand on hip, one hand pointing or gesturing, wick blazing, grinning directly at the viewer. This is the primary packaging and identity pose.
2. **Running pose** — mid-stride, wick streaming behind, arms pumping, expression: gleeful
3. **Triumphant pose** — arms raised, wick at full height, grin maximum, feet off the ground slightly
4. **Scheming pose** — leaning forward, hands pressed together, one eyebrow raised (if expressible), wick coiling
5. **Surprised pose** — eyes massive, wick extinguished momentarily (a flat thin line at the top), arms flung back

**Required expression sheet (front-facing head only, 6 expressions):**
1. Default grin — baseline
2. Ecstatic — eyes huge, wick blazing tall
3. Sneaky — one eye narrowed, grin slightly asymmetric
4. Furious — eyebrows (suggest them through eye shape and angle), teeth bared differently
5. Alarmed — eyes extremely wide, wick flickering
6. Smug — eyes half-lidded, grin unchanged, head tilted slightly

**Turnaround:**
- Front view (full body)
- 3/4 view (primary storytelling angle — this is the most used view)
- Side view
- Back view (minimal but needed for reference)

**Deliverables:**
- Full character sheet at A2 size, 300dpi
- All poses separated on transparent backgrounds
- All expressions on transparent backgrounds
- Layered source file (PSD or AI) with body parts on separate layers for animation preparation

**File formats:** AI or PSD (layered), PNG (each pose and expression, transparent background)

### 1.4 — Texture & Pattern System

**Film grain overlay:**
A seamless, tileable grain texture at three densities: light (15%), medium (20%), heavy (30%). Used as multiply or overlay layer on all finished artwork. Should feel like 16mm film stock, not digital noise.

**Halftone pattern:**
A seamless, tileable halftone dot pattern in two scales: fine (for detail areas) and coarse (for large background zones). Warm dot color, transparent background.

**Burn/scorch mark elements:**
A set of 8–12 isolated scorch mark / burn edge graphics. Irregular, organic shapes that look like heat damage on paper. Used as decorative dividers, edge treatments, and section breaks across all brand materials. Vary in size and intensity.

**Kraft paper texture:**
A high-resolution (300dpi minimum) tiling kraft paper texture. Warm, fibrous, slightly uneven tone. Used as base layer for all packaging and print applications.

**Deliverables:**
- All textures as seamless tiling TIFFs at 300dpi
- Scorch marks as individual transparent PNGs and as an AI file set
- All textures also provided as Photoshop pattern presets (.pat)

### 2.1 — Character Sheets: Remaining 7

Each character requires the same deliverable set as Blaze:
- Full character sheet (hero pose + 4 poses + 6 expressions)
- Turnaround (front, 3/4, side, back)
- Layered source file for animation preparation

**MATCHSTICK**

Body: Long, thin vertical form. Head is a rounded matchstick tip — pale sulphur yellow at rest, ignites orange-white when emotional state is high. Body is the matchstick stick — a warm off-white/cream cylinder, very narrow. Limbs are proportionally thin even by rubber-hose standards.

Proportions: Taller than wide by a large margin. The opposite of Ha-Hank. If standing next to Blaze, Matchstick is visibly the thinnest character in the room.

Movement character: Twitchy, quick, snapping between poses rather than flowing through them. The design should suggest this even in static poses — weight on balls of feet, slightly forward lean, coiled energy.

Color:
- Body/stick: Pale Cream `#E8E0A0`
- Head (unlit): Pale Sulphur `#D4CC80`
- Head (lit): Ember Orange `#E8621A` outer, Aged Cream `#F2E4C4` hottest center (same flame treatment as Blaze's wick)
- Shadow: `#B8A860` (warm shadow on body)
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410`

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — mid-run, slightly frantic, arms wide, head lit
2. Alert/listening pose — completely still, eyes wide, head unlit
3. Fast delivery pose — blur lines on limbs, racing somewhere
4. Relieved pose — slightly wilted, head flame small and flickering
5. Sneaking pose — exaggerated tiptoe, head unlit, eyes enormous

---

**HA-HANK**

Body: Wide, rounded habanero body — more horizontal than vertical. Stocky. Built. He fills space. Head merges naturally with the body in the Fleischer style — he is round from every angle.

Proportions: Noticeably wider and heavier than other characters. Shortest character except Matchstick. If standing in a group, he is the widest silhouette.

Movement character: Slow, deliberate, heavy. His animations have weight — when he moves, things nearby react. Default energy: completely relaxed. He appears slow until something requires him not to be.

Color:
- Body: Deep Orange `#D45000`
- Highlight zone: Ember Orange `#E8621A` (top-facing surfaces)
- Shadow: `#8B2800` (deep warm shadow, hard edge)
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410`
- Eyes: Standard — Aged Cream sclera, Char Black iris, slightly smaller than other characters' eyes relative to head size

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — arms wide open, enormous grin, completely at ease
2. Laughing pose — head back, hands on belly, silent cartoon laugh
3. Ready pose — slight forward lean, arms loose, still smiling (this is his "about to do something" pose — looks the same as relaxed to everyone except him)
4. Post-action pose — something behind him is on fire or destroyed, he looks at the viewer, still smiling
5. Thinking pose — one finger to chin, looking up, grin unchanged, clearly not deeply troubled by whatever he's thinking about

**CAROLINA**

Body: Elongated pepper form — taller and more vertical than Blaze. The distinctive Carolina Reaper tail is stylized into a curved element that trails from her lower body — it functions like a dramatic cape or train rather than a literal pepper tail. It has presence and movement.

Proportions: The most vertically elegant character. Upright posture always. She does not slouch, lean casually, or collapse into rubber-hose looseness the way others do. Her rubber-hose quality comes through in the tail and in subtle arm movement — her base posture is composed.

Movement character: Every movement is intentional. No wasted motion. She arrives at a position and holds it. The tail moves independently and dramatically — it is her most expressive element.

Color:
- Body: Crimson `#991100`
- Tail/train: grades from Crimson into Deep Crimson `#5C0A00` at the tip
- Highlight: Fire Red `#CC2200` on top-facing curves
- Shadow: Near black `#2A0500`, hard edge
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410` — slightly thinner than Blaze's outline weight, suggesting precision
- Eyes: Aged Cream sclera, Char Black iris — slightly narrower and more horizontal than other characters (deliberate, calm gaze)

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — standing straight, arms at sides or one arm extended slightly, looking directly at viewer, tail curved elegantly behind her
2. Observing pose — looking at something off-frame, slight tilt of head, expression unreadable
3. The moment before — completely still, tail rising slightly, something is about to happen
4. Dismissing pose — one hand raised slightly, palm outward, looking away, tail sweeping in the opposite direction
5. Rare smile pose — the smile that actually reaches her eyes. Used sparingly in content. Significant when it appears.

**GHOST**

Body: A pepper form that is never fully solid. Semi-transparent — the character must be rendered with visible transparency layers from the start. Below the waist, his form dissolves into vapor rather than having defined legs and feet. He drifts.

Proportions: Standard height but the transparent lower half means he reads as slightly taller and less grounded than others. His outlines are thinner than the other characters — suggesting he is less present.

Movement character: Drifts. Does not walk. Appears in positions without being seen in transit. His animations should cut from one position to another rather than showing movement between them — or show a ghostly blur trail.

Color:
- Body: Ghost White `#E8EBE4` primary
- Transparency zones: multiply layer at 40–60% — the background shows through his lower form
- Highlight: Pure white `#FFFFFF` (the only character where pure white is appropriate — it suggests luminosity)
- Shadow: Pale Blue-Grey `#C5CDD0`, very subtle, hard edge but low contrast
- Outline: `#5A6060` — the outline is dark but not the full Char Black weight. Lighter, suggesting his reduced solidity.
- Eyes: dimmer than other characters — the shine highlight on his eyes is smaller. They glow faintly rather than reflecting brightly.
- Vapor trail: starts at the waist zone, shifts from Ghost White to transparent over 30–40% of the lower body

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — floating slightly above the baseline, arms at sides, looking directly at the viewer with that dim gaze
2. Background presence — the exact same pose, but at 60% opacity, designed to be placed in the background of group scenes
3. Appearing pose — materializing out of vapor, lower half still dispersed, upper half solidifying
4. Disappearing pose — the reverse
5. Behind someone pose — positioned directly behind another character (sized for Blaze), expression unreadable

Special instruction: Ghost's hero pose should work at low opacity in the background of other characters' compositions. Deliver a 60% opacity version specifically for this use.

**ABI**

Body: Small, round pea form. Green, compact, self-contained. Unlike the loose rubber-hose quality of the pepper characters, Abi's design has slightly more geometric precision — her limbs still have the rubber-hose fluidity but her overall silhouette is cleaner and more considered. She wears a simple functional wrap or band around her midsection — neutral in color, no decoration.

Proportions: One of the smaller characters. Next to Blaze, she is noticeably compact. This is not a disadvantage in her design — compactness reads as density and precision.

Movement character: Economy. Every gesture has a purpose. She does not fidget. Her resting state is completely still and evaluating.

Color:
- Body: Wasabi Green `#7DB050`
- Shadow: Deep Green `#2D5016`, hard cel-shaded edge
- Highlight: Yellow-Green `#A8CC60` on top-facing curves
- Wrap/band: Smoke `#7A6E5F`
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410`
- Eyes: Aged Cream sclera, Char Black iris — focused, slightly narrowed, as if always mid-assessment

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — arms folded, weight balanced, looking at something with full attention
2. Analysis pose — one hand raised, one finger extended, explaining something
3. Action pose — in motion toward something with complete commitment, no hesitation in the body
4. Waiting pose — completely still, arms at sides, standing in a doorway or at the edge of a scene
5. Satisfied pose — a single small nod. Almost invisible change from the hero pose. This is her version of triumphant.

**SHIPKA**

Body: Stocky, broad-shouldered pepper form. Shipka pepper proportions — rounder and shorter than the elongated chilis, which distinguishes him immediately from Blaze and Carolina. Visible texture marks or scar details on his surface (healed, not fresh — he has been through things and they did not break him). He may wear a simple garment or accessory that suggests a working person — not decorative.

Proportions: The widest-standing character after Ha-Hank, but where Ha-Hank is round, Shipka is solid. He looks like he could hold a wall up.

Movement character: Deliberate. He has nowhere to rush. When he moves it is with full intention. He is the only character who stands completely still by choice and looks natural doing it.

Color:
- Body: Bulgarian Red `#A03020` (a slightly more muted, deeper red than Blaze — he is older, more settled)
- Highlight: `#C84030` on top-facing surfaces
- Shadow: `#501510`, hard edge, heavier shadow weight than younger characters
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410` — heaviest outline weight in the cast. He has the most solid presence.
- Eyes: Aged Cream sclera, Char Black iris — calm, direct, looking at the viewer without agenda. He holds eye contact.
- Scar details: rendered as slightly raised texture marks within his outline, in a darker variant of his body color

Optional accessory: A simple worn cloth at his neck or a weathered apron — something that says he works with his hands and always has. Keep it simple — one element, no color beyond the palette.

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — standing straight, arms at sides, looking directly at the viewer. No gesture. No lean. Just presence.
2. Mentor pose — one large hand on a smaller character's shoulder (design to work with Matchstick or Blaze positioned alongside)
3. Observing pose — arms folded, watching something off-frame, expression calm but attentive
4. Memory pose — looking at something not in the frame, expression softer, more inward
5. The moment of action — he rarely acts but when he does there is no hesitation. One deliberate pose, weight shifted forward, expression unchanged.

Special instruction: Shipka should be visually readable as the oldest member of the cast. This is achieved through outline weight, proportion solidity, and the scars/texture — not through exaggerated age caricature. He is not a comedy old man. He is a veteran.

**SMOKY**

Body: Smoked paprika form — a wider, disc-like pepper body, flatter than the round chilis. Always partially obscured by his own smoke, which is not an effect applied to him but part of his design from the first line. The smoke emerges from him naturally and is present in every pose — it is as much part of his silhouette as his arms.

Proportions: Medium height, medium width. The smoke adds to his perceived size without making him physically large. He occupies space in a different way — through atmosphere rather than volume.

Movement character: The smoke moves when he does and continues moving after he stops. His character animation is slower than anyone else's. He leans — on walls, counters, doorframes. He is rarely in the center of the frame.

Color:
- Body: Deep Burgundy `#5C1F1F`
- Highlight: `#8B3A3A` on top-facing surfaces
- Shadow: `#2A0A0A`, very deep, hard edge
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410`
- Smoke: Smoke `#7A6E5F`, with transparent edges — the smoke dissipates as it moves away from his body. Rendered as a shape, not a photographic effect.
- Eyes: Aged Cream sclera, Char Black iris — half-lidded. Never fully open. This is his permanent expression and it reads as cool, not tired.

Poses needed:
1. Hero pose — leaning against something (provide a simple surface or architectural element as context), smoke rising, eyes half-lidded, looking at something off to the side
2. Moving pose — walking through smoke, hands in pockets equivalent, completely unhurried
3. Arriving pose — smoke clears slightly, he steps through it. First impression pose.
4. Speaking pose — barely moving, one hand raised slightly, smoke curling from the gesture
5. The one time he runs — urgency that contrasts completely with his normal energy. Eyes fully open (this is notable). Smoke streaming behind.

Special instruction: Deliver the smoke as a separate layer in the source file. It will be animated independently of the body in motion work.

### 2.2 — Tattoo Flash Sheet

**What it is:** A single A2 (or A3) print featuring all 8 characters arranged as traditional tattoo flash wall art. This is both a merch product (sold as a poster) and the proof-of-concept that the character designs are genuinely tattoo-quality, not just tattoo-adjacent.

**Layout:**
All 8 characters arranged on a single sheet in the manner of a tattoo studio flash wall — characters placed at varied scales and slight angles, as if hand-pinned. Not a rigid grid. Not a poster layout. It should look like it belongs on the wall of a traditional tattoo parlour.

- Each character is rendered in their hero pose, isolated (no background)
- Character name in hand-lettered style beneath each figure
- The Fire Crackers seal in one corner — small, unobtrusive
- Aged Cream or off-white background with light kraft texture — the paper should look like flash paper
- Scorch mark accents at edges and corners

**Scale test:** Before finalising any character for the flash sheet, the design must be legible and clean at 5cm tall. Print a test at that size. If detail is lost or outlines merge, the character design needs simplification. This is the tattoo flash standard — every design must survive reduction.

**Style note:** Study Sailor Jerry Collins flash sheets directly. The organisation, the variety of scale, the way negative space is used between characters. The Fire Crackers sheet should feel like it belongs in the same tradition — not as a pastiche, but as a contemporary work in the same lineage.

**Deliverables:**
- Flat print-ready PDF at A2, 300dpi, CMYK
- Digital version at 3000px wide, RGB, PNG (for online store)
- Each character isolated at flash-sheet scale as individual PNG (for potential future use as stickers, individual prints)

### 3.2 — The Factory Environment

Background design for the mini-series primary setting.

**The Factory:**
A converted old tobacco or food factory. Warm, slightly chaotic, lived-in. Brick walls, high windows with dramatic light shafts, cluttered workbenches covered with ingredients and equipment. A large central oven/kiln that glows at all times. Handwritten signs on the walls. A map of Bulgaria with distribution routes marked. A "Wall of Flavors" where new recipes are posted and crossed out.

Deliver:
- Full factory interior, wide shot (1920×1080, layered by depth zone for parallax)
- Close-up zones: workbench, oven face, entrance door, the Wall of Flavors
- Day and night versions (different window light)
- All elements as separate layers for camera movement and character interaction

**Style:** Same retro animation style as characters. Backgrounds in Cuphead use a painterly approach — warm, textured, slightly dreamlike. Reference Cuphead casino and forest backgrounds for texture density and color complexity.

### 3.3 — Mr. Bland Character Sheet

The antagonist. A plain, unsalted cracker in a corporate suit.

**Design:**
- Body: literal flat cracker shape — square with rounded corners, pale beige, dot perforation pattern on surface (the classic cracker grid)
- The cracker body IS the torso and head combined — a flat square with a face on it, limbs emerging from the corners
- Suit: charcoal grey, perfectly pressed, no variation in color or texture
- Expression: neutral, always. He does not emote in the way characters emote. His face has the minimum number of features necessary to read as a face.
- Briefcase: always present, always closed, always exactly parallel to his leg

**Color:**
- Body: Pale Cracker `#E8D8B0` — the most desaturated, lifeless warm tone in the palette
- Shadow: `#C8B888` — barely darker
- Suit: `#4A4A4A`
- Outline: Char Black `#1C1410` — same weight as other characters, but it feels wrong on him, like he borrowed it

**Poses needed:**
- Standard pose (walking, briefcase in hand, expression neutral)
- Filing a form (bent slightly at the clipboard, still neutral)
- Observing from a distance (slightly behind a corner, expression neutral)
- The moment he smiles (one pose, used once per season — it is deeply unsettling)

## Technical Requirements (All Assets)

- **Color mode:** RGB for digital assets, CMYK for print assets. Provide both where relevant.
- **Resolution:** 300dpi minimum for all print assets. 72dpi at 2× scale for digital.
- **File naming:** `FC_[AssetType]_[CharacterOrVariant]_[Version].ext` — e.g., `FC_Character_Blaze_HeroPose_v1.png`
- **Version control:** Deliver v1 for review before finalizing. Label clearly.
- **Layered source files:** All character and packaging files must be delivered with layers intact and named. Flattened PNGs are export-only.
- **Font files:** Include or specify all fonts used. No system fonts embedded without specification.
- **Color references:** All fills must use exact hex codes from the brand palette. Specify in a color swatch file delivered with assets.

## Notes for the Designer

This brand lives or dies on the quality and consistency of the character art. The style is demanding — it requires genuine craft in line quality, not just knowing what Cuphead looks like. Before beginning, confirm you have studied the Fleischer Studios and Cuphead references thoroughly.

The characters are adults with adult personalities. Do not soften them toward cute. The grin on Blaze is the grin of someone who knows exactly what they're about to do to your taste buds and considers this a gift.

When in doubt: go bolder. Every version of "should this line be heavier" should be answered yes.
